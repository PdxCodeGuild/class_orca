The Life of Muhammad Ali


Muhammad Ali was an American professional boxer, a staunch activist, and a cultural icon. Finishing his professional boxing career with a record of 56-5, his nickname was the greatest and he is considered by many to be the greatest boxer of all time. He received great notoriety for refusing to be drafted into the military and fighting in the Vietnam War due to his religious beliefs. He was arrested for draft evasion and stripped of his titles though the conviction was overturned at a later time. Ali eventually was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease and eventually succumbed to it many years later at the age of 74. Even after his death, his impact on culture globally can still be felt due to the incredible life that he led.
